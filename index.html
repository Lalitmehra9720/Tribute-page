ABOUT:

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. He studied physics and aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology, graduating in 1960. Kalam began his career as a scientist at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO), where he worked on the development of missiles and nuclear weapons. He played a key role in the development of India’s missile program, earning him the nickname “Missile Man.” He also served as the project head of India’s first Satellite Launch Vehicle, which successfully launched the Rohini satellite into orbit in 1980.

achievement

Renowned as the “Missile Man of India,” Kalam was a prominent scientist and statesman. He played a pivotal role in India’s civilian space program and military missile development.

biography

Kalam served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007, elected against Lakshmi Sehgal with support from both the Bharatiya Janata Party and the Indian National Congress.Throughout his life, Abdul Kalam was recognized for his contributions to India’s scientific and technological advancements. He remained a respected figure until his passing, inspiring generations with his vision for a self-reliant India.
